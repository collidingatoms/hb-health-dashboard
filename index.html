TSH — Thyroid Stimulating Hormone

⚠ Expert Assessment

TSH at 62.7 — this is severe hypothyroidism and a medical emergency-level reading. Normal TSH is 0.55–4.78 uIU/mL. Your TSH has exploded from 9.3 (Jul 2024) to 62.7 (Aug 2025), a 6.7x increase in 13 months. This means your thyroid is barely functioning — confirmed by Free T4 at 0.69 (below 0.83 minimum) and Free T3 at 2.26 (below 2.3 minimum). Severe hypothyroidism worsens everything: it raises cholesterol, causes insulin resistance (making diabetes harder to control), causes fatigue, weight gain, brain fog, and depression. T3 has been consistently low since 2023. You need levothyroxine (or dose increase if already prescribed) immediately, with recheck in 6–8 weeks.
