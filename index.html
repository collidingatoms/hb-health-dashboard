Urine Routine — Apr 2026 Snapshot

Assessment — Apr 2026

Two abnormal findings, both explainable. Glucose 3+ (heavy spill) is consistent with the fasting 147 mg/dL reading — kidneys excrete glucose when blood levels exceed the renal threshold (~180 mg/dL at random). This will resolve as HbA1c and fasting drop further.

Ketones 2+ is new — absent in Aug 2025. Moderate ketonuria in a diabetic can mean several things: prolonged fasting before the test, low-carb / ketogenic eating, aggressive weight loss, or (the worrying one) insufficient insulin driving lipolysis. Given everything else is improving, this is most likely benign — but flag to the doctor to rule out diabetic ketoacidosis tendency, especially pair with a capillary ketone check if fasting sugars spike again. Everything else — protein, blood, leucocyte esterase, nitrite, pus cells, RBC — normal.

Track 1 — Diabetic Retinopathy. The OCT Macula is clean. Central retinal thickness 282 µm R / 264 µm L (well within the 250–320 µm normal band); foveal minimum 230 µm R / 218 µm L. No cystoid macular edema, no subretinal fluid, no thickening pattern suggestive of Diabetic Macular Edema. This is reassuring given 20 years of diabetes and HbA1c 11.8%. However, OCT alone does not rule out early non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy — a dilated fundus photograph is still needed to close out the DR question properly. Ask for this at the next visit.

Track 2 — Glaucoma-suspect. The structural and functional findings together form a glaucoma-suspect picture in the right eye. Cup-to-Disc (vertical) is 0.74 R / 0.76 L (a C/D ≥0.6 is a yellow flag; ≥0.7 is suspicious). DDLS 4 and 5 place both discs in the possible-early-damage band. The GCL+IPL minimum thickness in the left eye is 23 µm (concerningly thin — this layer is where glaucomatous damage shows up first), though the mean is preserved (82 µm). The HVF 24-2 for the right eye shows GHT Outside Normal Limits with PD (pattern deviation) at 0.47 and a cluster of defects visible on the probability maps, while the left eye HVF is within normal limits. This R-eye VF + elevated C/D combination is exactly the profile Dr Pandey is now trying to confirm or exclude with a 1-month repeat HVF.

What's missing from the workup: intraocular pressure (IOP) — essential for the glaucoma picture; a dilated fundus photograph — essential to complete the DR read; pachymetry (corneal thickness) — affects IOP interpretation. All three should be requested at the follow-up visit on 1 May 2026.

Prescription (31 Mar 2026): Tab Forslin — 1×/day × 30 days (after food) · Eye Drops TIZTA — 3×/day, both eyes · Gel Cladra — 1×/day at night, both eyes. Repeat HVF at the 1-month mark is the single most important next step; glaucoma field defects are often reproducible on repeat testing, and a second consistent pattern moves this from suspect to diagnosed.
